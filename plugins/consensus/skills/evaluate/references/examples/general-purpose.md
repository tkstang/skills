# General-Purpose Evaluation Rubric

Use this template as a starting point for evaluating most written artifacts. Adapt the criteria and weights to the specific artifact type and goals before running the wrapper.

> **Authoring note:** The headings below are the machine-parsed criteria. The wrapper extracts `##`–`######` headings and `-`/`*` bullets, dedupes, and caps at 12. Scoring scales and guidance paragraphs are peer-facing only.

---

## Clarity

The artifact communicates its core message without ambiguity. The reader should not need to guess at intent.

_Weight: high. Scoring: 0 = unclear throughout; 1 = mostly unclear; 2 = mostly clear with gaps; 3 = clear throughout._

## Completeness

The artifact covers all necessary elements for its stated purpose. Key information is not missing or deferred without explanation.

_Weight: high._

## Accuracy

Claims, data references, and technical details are correct and consistent with each other.

_Weight: high._

## Structure and Organization

The artifact is logically organized. Related content is grouped; the progression from beginning to end is coherent.

_Weight: medium._

## Conciseness

The artifact avoids unnecessary repetition and filler. Every section earns its place.

_Weight: medium._

## Audience Fit

The tone, vocabulary, and assumed knowledge level match the intended audience.

_Weight: medium._

## Actionability

Where action is called for, the artifact makes it clear what the next steps are and who is responsible.

_Weight: medium._

## Evidence and Support

Claims are supported by reasoning, examples, data, or citations as appropriate to the context.

_Weight: medium._

## Consistency

Terminology, formatting, and style are consistent throughout the artifact.

_Weight: low._

## Accessibility

The artifact is navigable: headings, summaries, or signposting help the reader find what they need.

_Weight: low._

---

**How to adapt this rubric**

1. Remove criteria that do not apply to your artifact type.
2. Add domain-specific criteria (keep the total at 12 or fewer headings/bullets).
3. Adjust the weight labels to match your priorities.
4. Add scoring examples beneath each criterion if the peers will benefit from concrete anchors.
